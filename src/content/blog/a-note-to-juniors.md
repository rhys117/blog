This isn't original, smarter people than me are already writing about this. So let me parrot their wisdom.

Software engineering isn't going anywhere. Code monkeys are another story though. Or I'm wrong and we'll be all out of a job in two years.

There is a big difference between vibe coding and augmented coding [^1]. Those that learn how to work in the loop with an agent and leverage 'the genie' will be the problem solvers our businesses rely on in the future.

Learn what the agent needs to thrive, the context, the design, and make sure you're choosing a sensible language [^3]. They thrive in an environment of less noise, and more signal.

What's working against you. Architectural knowledge takes time, it takes care, and it takes effort. There are those already saying it doesn't matter anymore. Maybe they're right. The minds I trust say otherwise though and I tend to agree.

Vibe projects start out with so much promise. Then they hit a certain size, the velocity slows and things start to break. Maybe nobody even knows how the thing actually works[^5]. The agent starts contradicting itself, and suddenly you’re fighting the tool instead of building with it. Design stamina remains true[^2].

![Design Stamina Hypothesis](/images/design_stamina.png)

I've personally seen agents do some funny things. Delete tests. Write useless tests. Do gymnastics to solve something that's a one liner. One deleted my entire home directory. Without architectural intent, you can't catch any of that. You don't even know it's wrong. That's the difference between vibe coding and augmented coding, and it's exactly why intent still matters.

Use AI to accelerate your learning. Use it with intent. If you fail to do so you won't be on the trajectory of 'good design' because you won't know what it is. Therein lies the risk, and the people in the industry that will be replaceable. Don't become a code monkey.

The good news, a lot of vibe coded projects are great ideas, and these fast prototypes could be an opportunity for many of us in the industry in the future. They'll need to scale, they'll need real architecture, and they'll need people who know how to maintain the velocity. The genie ***and you*** are still the answer to that. There will always be jobs for the problem solvers in the industry, in fact they might just be more in demand than ever, will they still be called programmers? Probably.

Get this right and you'll outpace the seniors in the industry that refuse to. And yes I'm going to quote Bane, we merely adopted the dark; you were born in it, molded by it.

We've been here before. 4GL, a concept that was going to take our jobs was the talk of the 80's. This was before my time so it's only hearsay to me, but alas we're still here[^4].
